It's usually served warm in tiny porcelain cups, but some trendy American restaurants served it chilled like white wine. Sake doesn't age well in the bottle and should be consumed within a year of bottling. Substitutes: Shaoxing wine (not as smooth and sweet) OR vermouth (dry) OR white wine OR sherry (dry)
