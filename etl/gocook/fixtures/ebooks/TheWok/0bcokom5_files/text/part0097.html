PAD KHANA BACON KROP (THAI-STYLE STIR-FRIED CHINESE BROCCOLI WITH OYSTER SAUCE AND BACON)

Yield

Serves 3 to 4

Active Time

10 minutes

Total Time

10 minutes

NOTE

For a more traditional version of this dish, replace the pancetta with 8 ounces (225 g) Crispy Fried Pork Belly (here), cut into ½-inch chunks.

INGREDIENTS

1 teaspoon (5 ml) peanut, rice bran, or other neutral oil

4 ounces (120 g) thick-cut pancetta or bacon, cut into ½-inch dice

5 medium garlic cloves, finely minced (about 5 teaspoons/15 g)

Fresh or pickled hot chiles, thinly sliced

12 ounces (340 g) Chinese broccoli (gai lan), washed and drained, leaves roughly chopped, stems cut on a sharp bias into 1- to 2-inch pieces

1½ tablespoons (25 ml) oyster sauce

1 tablespoon (15 ml) dark soy sauce or shoyu

1 tablespoon (12.5 g) sugar

Pad khana moo krop is a Thai reinterpretation of Chinese broccoli with oyster sauce. The classic dish combines chunks of crispy pork belly with briefly stir-fried Chinese broccoli flavored with chiles, garlic, and oyster sauce. I rarely go through the trouble of deep frying my own pork belly just for the sake of this dish (though if I have leftovers of the fried pork belly from here, I use it!). Instead, I use thick-sliced bacon or pancetta, which, while by no means traditional, is undeniably delicious, and it cuts the total time from fridge to table down to around 10 minutes. Perfect for a harried weeknight.

Unlike Chinese-style Chinese broccoli with oyster sauce, in this version the broccoli is thinly sliced and very briefly stir-fried to retain a nearly raw crunch when served.

DIRECTIONS

Heat the oil in a wok over high heat until shimmering. Add the pancetta or bacon and cook, stirring, until well rendered and crisp, 1 to 2 minutes. Add the garlic and chiles to taste and stir-fry until fragrant, about 15 seconds. Add the Chinese broccoli and stir-fry for 15 seconds. Add the oyster sauce, soy sauce, and sugar and stir-fry until lightly wilted, about 30 seconds. Transfer to a serving platter, and serve.
